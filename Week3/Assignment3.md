## Step 2 — Research & Write 
For your chosen field, answer the following in essay form, organized with headers:  

*Look for core concepts in the job descriptions. Look for keywords like Object-Oriented Programming (OOP), Memory Management, Pointers, Data Structures, or File I/O, and see if those show up in your chosen field's requirements.*

1. Field overview. What does someone in this role actually do day to day? 
    - What problems do they solve? (Pull from at least one real job posting and one article or industry source.)    

2. Required skills and tools. 
    - Programming languages, frameworks, libraries, and tools commonly required. 
    - Which of these overlap with what's being taught in this course, and which are entirely new?  

3. Connection to C++. 
    - Is C++ actually used in this field, and if so, how/why? 
    - If C++ isn't commonly used, what is the dominant language, and why?  

4. Educational path. 
    - What degrees, certifications, or self-taught paths do people in this field typically follow? 
    - Are bootcamps or certifications respected in this area, or is a CS degree expected?  

5. Three portfolio projects. Propose three specific projects a student could build to demonstrate readiness for this field — ideally ranging from "doable in the next semester or two" to "more ambitious, capstone-level."
    - For each: 
        - Briefly describe what it would involve?
        - What skills/tools it would use?
        - Why it would be compelling to an employer in this field?

    For your first project ('doable in the next semester'), explain how you could build it using the foundational C++ concepts you know right now (e.g., loops, functions, classes, arrays, or file processing) before transitioning to complex frameworks.    

6. Target companies. 
    - Identify 3–5 companies that hire for this role and explain why each is a good fit — company size, culture, what they're known for building, growth/learning opportunities, or specific things their career pages or engineering blogs say they value. 
   
    - Identify 3 internship roles (either currently open or past listings archived online) and list the top 3 technical requirements for each. 

    - Find 1 person on LinkedIn that is already in the role. Check out their experiences and look at their resume. (**BONUS:** Send a connection request with a nice note introducing yourself!)

    **Go beyond the obvious big names (Meta, Anthropic, Nvidia, Google, OpenAI) and include at least one mid-size or local/regional company.**  

7. Career outlook. 
    - What's the job market like for this field: demand, growth projections, typical salary ranges?  
    Cite a source like the BLS Occupational Outlook Handbook, levels.fyi, or a recent industry report.  

8. Personal reflection. 
- Why does this field interest you? 
- What skills do you already have a head start on? 
- What's the biggest gap?  
- What's one concrete thing you could do this semester to move toward it?

## Step 3 — Format & Submission

800–1,200 words words (roughly 2–4 pages double-spaced), with section headers matching the prompts above!  
At **least 5 credible sources**, cited, with a reference list.  
Sources should include at least one real job posting, one company career/engineering page, and one labor-market/industry source — not just blog opinion pieces!  

Submit **PDF** on Brightspace.
